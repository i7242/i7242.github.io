A simple tolerance synthesis example is conducted. The tolerance analysis is based on Jacobian-Torsor mordel. Genetic Algorithm (GA) is used to minimize the manufacturing cost while the precision requirements are guaranteed.
The used GA is from DEAP: https://github.com/DEAP/deap

Analysis Model

The model used here is a simple model, which the optimization result can be expected.
This is helpful to verify the optimization result. The model is shown as below:

The Part A (blue color) is assumed to be fixed, its interial cylinder surface a has translation and rotation deviations relative to the center point o . The center point o is the origen of local coordinate system of cylinder surface a . To make the analysis easy, center points of features are illustrated in the figure. The axes of local coordinate systems are aligned whith the local coordinate system of cylinder surface a . However, to avoid to many arrows in the figure, they are not shown.

Cylinder surface b on Part B is assumed to mate with cylinder surface a . There exist no gaps between them. The functional dimensions are the position and orientation of cylinder surface c on the other hand of Part B. Surface c has deviation relative to surface b . To be simple to explain the optimization result, we assume Part A and Part B cost the same for certain precision value (e.g., they have same cost function).

The statistical characteristics, mean and standard deviation, are used to evaluate the part/assembly precision (or manufacturing deviation). Thousands of simulations will be conducted. Therefore the first step is to define a function to conduct this simulation and return the results.

Precision Requirements

The precision requirements are applied on the deviation of cylinder T_{c/o} . They are considered as constraints in the optimization problem. In Genetic Algorithm provided in DEAP, penalty functions are used to introduce these constraints.

Here we just give an example of the devination of requirements. They should be defined in “feasible” function.

Cost-Tolerance Functions

For cost function, its shape and parameters are importent.
The used function is shown as below. Detailed parameters need to be determined.

More cost functions can be established, and the princple and method are the same.
Additionally, the cost for rotational precision is scaled by 1e^3 . Alternatively, defferent cost functions can be introduced for different torsor variables.

Constraints

The constraints contains two parts:

  • the precision requirements on functional dimensions
    • is the calculated dev_{sigma} is larger than dev_{lmt} , it violate the constraint
  • the precision requirements on functional elements
    • the upper bound of functional element deviation is expressed in precision requirement implicitly
    • the upper bound of functional element deviation is also influenced by individual initialization (due to the algorithm and detaild configurations)
    • the lower bound of functional element deviation is added as constraint, science:
      • the limitation of the cost function we use. it give a large value when the deviation sigma is zero (perferct condition). however infact is should be infinitely large. that is, cost function has a significant interval
      • the individuals are not totally random generated. to accelerate the convergence of the algorithm, they are initialized in a reasonable value based on engineering experience

Genetic Algorithm

This is the implementation of Genetic Algorithm to conduct optimization.
For more details and explaination, please visit their websites.

Page of example:
http://deap.readthedocs.io/en/master/examples/ga_onemax.html
Add constraints by penalty function:
https://deap.readthedocs.io/en/master/tutorials/advanced/constraints.html?highlight=constraint

Several times of optimization may be conducted and chose suitable solution among them.
This is due to:

  • the uncertainty in Genetic Algorithm
  • the uncertainty in tolerance analysis method

Optimization Result

  • Part A and Part B have similar standard deviation for translation torsor variable. However, Part A need to increase its orientation precision to guarantee the functional requirements are satisfied, when reducing the cost.Compared to Part A, the orientation deviation on Part B is less significant.
  • Currently, all the value of parameters are given roughly corresponding to experience. Therefor the result only shows the trends.
